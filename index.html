QSSSA

A Structured Conversation Strategy for Deeper Learning

1. Question

  • Pose an open-ended question
  • Incorporate rich vocabulary
  • Keep the question visible
Click for Details

Deeper Dive: Question

Why it works: An effective question is the catalyst for critical thinking. It sets the purpose for the conversation and challenges students to move beyond surface-level answers.

Example: Instead of "What was the main character's name?", ask "How did the character's decision at the beginning of the story influence the final outcome?"

2. Signal

  • Provide silent thinking time
  • Students use a non-verbal signal
  • Wait for high participation
Click for Details

Deeper Dive: Signal

Why it works: This step ensures equity of voice. It gives all students, especially introverted thinkers or language learners, the time they need to formulate their thoughts before speaking.

Pro-Tip: Establish a clear, consistent signal (e.g., thumbs-up on their chest) and hold students accountable for using it. This builds a classroom culture of thoughtful participation.

3. Stems

  • Provide academic sentence stems
  • Model how to use the stems
  • Connect stems to the question
Click for Details

Deeper Dive: Stems

Why it works: Stems provide a scaffold for academic language, helping students articulate their ideas clearly and professionally. They are a bridge from informal thought to formal discourse.

Examples: "I infer that..." for making a conclusion; "A different perspective is..." for disagreeing respectfully; "Based on the evidence, I conclude..." for analysis.

4. Share

  • Students share in pairs/triads
  • Encourage active listening
  • Low-stakes practice opportunity
Click for Details

Deeper Dive: Share

Why it works: Small group sharing builds confidence and allows every student to verbalize their thinking. It's a rehearsal for whole-group discussion and a chance to refine ideas with peers.

Pro-Tip: Give clear roles during sharing, such as Speaker 1, Speaker 2, and a "Builder" who adds on to the ideas. This promotes focused conversation.

5. Assess

  • Randomly call on students
  • Listen for understanding
  • Provide specific feedback
Click for Details

Deeper Dive: Assess

Why it works: This is a formative assessment checkpoint. It makes student thinking visible to the whole class and allows the teacher to identify trends, clarify misconceptions, and plan next steps.

Pro-Tip: Use this as an opportunity to celebrate strong academic language and critical thinking. Amplify student voices who effectively used the sentence stems or provided unique insights.
